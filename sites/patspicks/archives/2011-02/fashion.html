September 24, 2008
Target news from Jenn Luin

The new makeup lines were selected by Target to put them directly in competition with Sephora.

The new lines are Pixi by Petra, NP Set by Napoleon Perdis, and JK by Jemma Kidd. Scandalous note: Napoleon was originally sold at Sephora - he pulled his line so he could sell it at Target, hoping to make bigger sales.

However, what I am SO excited about are two accessories collaborations. Sigerson Morrison is doing a small line of shoes, very sparkly and festive- some are VERY young looking, but a few would be a nice shoe for holiday parties for your readers at bargain prices. The other collaboration is a line of handbags by Anya Hindmarch. In my opinion, these look to be the classiest design collaboration Target has done yet. Lots of patent for fall, but classic, ladylike shapes.

They debut online and in stores on October 12, and you'd better believe I'll be there! The big black tote and matching clutch are the ones I'm eyeing.
 
September 10, 2008
Save Our Suit

Patti Obenhaus passes along her suit tips from the Carlisle Collection. I know it's tempting to take off a “less-than-immaculate” suit and toss it in the dry cleaning pile but unless it is stained, it may only need to be steamed.

Save money and your suit! Ironing can flatten lapels and make some fabrics shiny. And don't forget to clean all pieces of the suit at the same time so they become worn at the same rate.

Patti will be showing the new collection September 18 through October 1.

What to Buy for Summer...
whether you’re going across the “Pond” or across town

1 Floral prints
2 Anything metallic
3 Soft, feminine skirts
4 Embellishment of any kind photo
5 Shirts with shape, shirt-jackets
6 Volume in jackets
7 Crystal beads and pearls
8 Appliqued Suits and piping
9 Pin striping in pants, narrow or wide

Thanks to Patti Obenhaus who will be showing these looks March 26 thru April 9th with the Summer Carlisle/per se Collection. Call Patti for an appointment at 327-9654.

Patti also has tips for clearing your closet for your new summer things. You know what we mean…. Wire hangers, ratty robes and slippers.

Now is the time to remove, rotate, and organize. Ban these from your closet:

1 Too many “work” clothes for painting and gardening
2 “Skinny” clothes
3 Shoes that are out of style or in disrepair
4 Frayed or old underwear
5 Single socks

Place bins on the top shelf to sort small items like gloves, hats, and scarves. Hang hooks on the inside wall or back of the closet door to hold purses nicely.
