Day 1 Simplify

Out with the many, in with the few. Overtreating your skin with multiple products is one of the most common problems Dr. Amy sees, so narrow down to just a few: a gentle, everyday facial cleanser; two good moisturizers -- one with sunscreen for day and another for night; and an exfoliant to use two or three times a week.

Do just two things, a.m. and p.m. In the morning, wash your face with your hands -- they're naturally thorough, but gentle. Apply daytime moisturizer to still-damp skin; let it soak in before applying makeup. Repeat at night, but spend a minute longer to be sure you remove all makeup, dirt, and residue from the day. Then, apply nighttime moisturizer.   

Establish a "get ready for bed" routine. How many hours of sleep do you need to feel really refreshed? If it’s 8, and you normally rise at 6 a.m., then call it a day by 9:00 p.m. That gives you an hour before your 10:00 p.m. sleeptime to wind down and wash up

Day 2 Relax

Connect with friends, book a massage, and/or have sex. Pick up the phone and call someone you haven’t spoken with in a while -- someone who makes you laugh and can take a load off your stress level and help you put things into perspective.

Start doing daily breathing exercises at least twice a day for the next 8 days, preferably at the same time. This will help reinforce a pattern and establish a breathing routine that calms you, refocuses your energy, and changes your body chemistry so that it supports happiness from the inside out.

Day 3 Go Green

Drink tea. As part of your Go Green day, make a big thermos of green tea at home, and sip it all day -- hot at first, over ice later, if you like. Try doing this every day this week.   

Go outside and take in nature. Make it a goal to spend at least 20 minutes outside today

Day 4  Eat Clean

Avoid fast food, fried food, and anything processed. Focus on eating as cleanly as possible -- no artificial or processed anything. Yes, that includes refined sugar, soda (diet and regular), and processed meats and cheeses

Day 5 Make a Move

Get active: Schedule at least 30 minutes of physical activity. Make today the day you do at least one thing (maybe two) more active than yesterday. Whether it’s taking a structured class at a gym, going for a power walk with friends, or dancing

Day 6 Foster Friendships

Plan a dinner with friends or family. Spend time today with people you care about. If you love to cook, invite them over for a scrumptious meal. If not, go out. The point is to get together with people who are meaningful in your life -- who make you laugh or inspire you -- and share stories and time.

Day 7 Pamper Yourself

Find some inner peace. In the hour prior to your bedtime tonight, try meditating. It’s a great way to work through stress, and it may also help you keep your brain young and functioning at its peak.

Day 8  Sleep-In Beauty

Sleep late, or try napping. No early obligations today? Sleep in as long as you like. If you can’t, try napping later in the afternoon. The ideal time is about 8 hours after you get up -- that’s when the body naturally takes a little dip in temperature (a sleep inducer); it’s part of your normal biological rhythm

Day 9 Reflect

How well has it gone? What helped the most?
