For light summer meals (and fast!):

TOMATO, BASIL & ROASTED CORN SALSA from Marilyn Scher
An outstanding topper for any grilled chicken or fish fillet.

Ingredients:
1 1/2 cups fresh corn kernels
1 pint cherry tomatoes, halved
1/4 cup sliced scallions (both green and white parts)
2 T. finely slivered fresh basil leaves
2 T. olive oil, divided
Salt, pepper

Preparation:
Heat a large skillet and add 1 tablespoon of olive oil. Add the corn and sauté over medium-high heat for about two minutes, or until corn is just beginning to color. Add in the tomatoes and the scallions, and cook for one minute more. Remove from heat and add the basil, the remaining tablespoon of olive oil, and salt and pepper to taste.

Celebrate the 4th with another kind of sparkler!

Celebrations call for sparkling wine and here is a selection of five types that are guaranteed to make the occasion special. Lee Ann Kocurek at Austin Wine Merchant recommends a Crémant de Bourgogne, the undervalued next door neighbor to the wines of Champagne. And what a difference that short distance makes in the price. A nice Simonnet-Febvre (chardonnay & varietals) costs $16.25.

From Spain there is the popular cava. Try Raventós ($19.99), A blend of 60% Macabeo, 20% Xarel-lo and 20% Parellada. Or an Italian Prosecco such as Loredan Gasparini ($13.75). A little more crisp and dry than most proseccos.

For an American wine, try Schramsberg Blanc de Noirs, a vintage sparkler at $29.99.

And, of course, the rock star of sparkling, Champagne. One of the best known Houses is Taittinger. There are many selections, but we picked the La Francaise Brut, NV, at $37.50.

The small growers are especially interesting because, unlike the large negotiants, they grow their own grapes. Try Gaston Chiquet Premier Cru at $45.

So perfect for summer, with hardly any effort required at all!
Grilled Vegetable Packets w/ Goat Cheese
from Marilyn Scher

3 small zucchini (about 1 lb.)

2 leeks, white and pale green parts only, halved lengthwise and then thinly sliced (wash well to get rid of excess dirt)

1/2 lb. asparagus spears, cut into 2" lengths

2 T. olive oil

Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper

2-3 T. crumbled goat cheese or ricotta salata


Prepare an outdoor grill to make a hot fire.  Toss all ingredients through the salt and pepper in a large mixing bowl.  Tear off two 24" sheets of heavy-duty aluminum foil; lay foil on flat surface and arrange half the vegetables on half of each piece of foil.  For each packet, fold other half of foil over vegetables and crimp edges all the way around to seal completely.  Grill the packets on a rack over a hot fire for about 20 minutes, until contents are sizzling and foil has puffed slightly.  Transfer packets to a large platter, and then carefully slit them open and pour contents out onto the platter.  Sprinkle with the crumbled cheese and serve.
 
This is an easy way to prepare vegetables and a fun alternative to the usual stovetop steaming.  Feel free to vary the selection of vegetables, being sure to cut them into appropriately sized chunks (harder vegetables such as carrots should be cut into smaller chunks than softer vegetables such as zucchini, etc.) so that all vegetables finish cooking in the same amount of time.  The hot fire of the grill will lightly caramelize the food as it steams inside the foil, intensifying the flavors without subjecting the food to any smoke.

Check out Marilyn's new Web site at www.marilynschercooking.com

Juanita's Ritas

Texas summer is fast approaching and things are getting ready to heat up! In order to stay cool, a Texas girl needs two things: a ridiculously flattering bathing suit that makes her feel like a Victoria's Secret model, and the perfect margarita to sip on by the pool. While finding the first item is like searching for the Holy Grail, the second can be made right in your kitchen! Here is a super quick and easy recipe for a delicious margarita that will keep you cool all summer long.

Using the small Minute Maid cans of limeade as a measuring cup, combine the following in a blender:
1 can Minute Maid limeade
1 can Tequila - Jose Cuervo Gold is fine. It's not the cheapest, but not the most expensive.
1 can Water
2 to 4 Tbsp Cointreau (Orange Liqueur)

Blend with ice and serve chilled with salt on the rim and a garnish of lime.
(Note: Do not try to use a store brand of limeade. Stick to Minute Maid.)

Marilyn Scher's Pound Cake Magic

Marilyn has been a cooking instructor for many years. What makes her classes unique is that class is a group of friends gathering in her kitchen. You gather friends and family and call Marilyn at 328-6622 (marilynscher@austin.rr.com) to select the menu; then meet in her Westlake kitchen to learn her secrets. This recipe for easy entertaining is from her column, which appears monthly in The Jewish Outlook (available at Randall's Westlake).

CITRUS-GLAZED POUND CAKE
One loaf pound cake, homemade or store-bought, topped with either of the
following:

Creamy Lemon Glaze:
1 1/4 cups confectioner's sugar
1/4 cup fresh lemon juice
Grated zest of 1 lemon
1/2 tsp. pure vanilla extract
Combine all ingredients and whisk until smooth.
Pour over cake and let set before slicing and serving.

Blood Orange Glaze:
1 cup fresh juice from blood oranges or tangerines
1/3 cup granulated sugar
1 Tbsp. unsalted butter
Combine all ingredients in small saucepan and cook over medium heat for about five minutes, or until sugar dissolves and mixture has thickened slightly. Pierce top of pound cake and pour glaze over entire surface until it is all absorbed into the cake.
Let cool before serving

Bella Vista Tour
Pick a glorious central Texas day for a drive, and head to Wimberly for a tour of the Bella Vista Ranch olive orchard. Enjoy a tour given by owner Jack Dougherty. He can tell you way more than you would ever think to ask about the history of olives, agriculture of olives, and about their many benefits. You will enjoy walking among the olive trees and learning of the different varieties, ways of processing, and uses for the olives.

You will learn how Bella Vista Ranch began and his other endeavors that go along with growing olives. In addition, he'll take you on a "comparison tour" of Mediterranean and Texas olive growing and the history of olive cultivation in both.

Following the approximately one-hour tour, Jack will introduce you to the processing methods, flavor variations and culinary uses of olive oil. He also produces wines, jams and jellies (samples included) from the fruits grown on the property.

So if you're looking for some place to go for a quick trip, try visiting the Bella Vista Ranch. It's an easy one hour drive from Austin. Check the website for driving directions and up-to-date tour information.

Tours: 10 AM and 1 PM on Saturday, 12 noon on Sunday. Group tours are also available by appointment.

Cost: $10 per person or the very entertaining full tour and tasting.

Website: www.bvranch.com

If you have a little more time available, a five-minute drive will take you into Wimberly for lunch and a little shopping.
