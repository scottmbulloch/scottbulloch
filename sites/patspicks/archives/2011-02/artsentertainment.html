Internationally acclaimed dancers perform with ARE

American Repertory Ensemble is only in its third season but already has established itself as a major player in the Austin arts scene. ARE is a core group of professional-caliber dancers and musicians from the Austin community. In addition, nationally known artists are invited from around the country to participate. "Passion at Play," the current production from ARE Artistic Director David Justin, features featuring principal dancers from the San Francisco Ballet, the New York City Ballet, and Twyla Tharp's "Moving Out."

The audience will travel the globe on a journey with jazz-influenced composer Michael Torke, the Spanish flavor of "Valses Poeticos," the British elegance of "The Dance House," and the American reinvention of the classic "L'Apres-midi d'un Faune." Tickets are $25.

In addition to the performances, on Friday night you may enjoy wine and hors d'ouvres at Vin Bistro and the opportunity to meet the artists before the performance ($65).

On Saturday night, Vin Bistro features a gourmet meal that follows the foods of the regions represented in the show, reserved seats for the performance, and the opportunity to meet the artists ($150).

What we're reading now

Katie Henry mentioned Three Cups of Tea: One Man's Mission to Promote Peace . . . One School at a Time, the amazing story of Greg Mortenson who became extremely ill when attempting to climb K2, the world's second tallest mountain, but became a leader in addressing poverty, educating girls, and overcoming cultural divides in Pakistan. Local villagers nursed him back to health, and he promised to return to build a school. He has now built over 50 schools in the wildest parts of Pakistan.

Jenn Luin gave me a fun, summer read, Somebody Is Going to Die if Lilly Beth Doesn't Catch That Bouquet. A complete guide to the perfect southern wedding, including finding a groom and staging the extravaganza. Includes recipes that take me back to another time and place!

Lang Lang, the controversial 26 year old pianist, has written the story of his rise to fame in Journey of a Thousand Miles. Lang Lang has played to sold out performances with the major orchestras of the world. He is known for theatrical performances and emotional responses. His was an improbable rise from a Chinese industrial city where his father determined that he would be the greatest pianist in the world.

June 11, 2008
Blogs R Us

Now that there are hundreds of millions of online blogs (online web logs, for those of you who've been living in a cave), odds are you know a blogger, you have a friend who's a blogger, or you've got your own blog. However it happened, welcome to the wonderful world of self-publishing – some of it is truly awful, full of grammatical errors and illogical thought; and some of it as beautifully written and photographed as Pulitzer-Prize winners.

The first blog I ever read, and still adore, is In The Pink Texas, written by Eileen Smith. She's the only blogger I know who has parlayed a blog into a day job. A former legislative staffer, Eileen is now the editor of Texas Monthly's online site, writing a blog for them about this year's presidential race called Poll Dancing. She's wickedly funny, and her cadre of commenters is hilarious too.

In most cases, comments make the blog better than it would be just by itself. It proves what good taste fellow fans of a blog have. Pat's Picks' blog, hosted on Blogger (a Google product) is set up so that Pat can read a comment before posting it. If means there will be a delay between when you comment and when the rest of the world can see it (Pat has a real job on the side, you know), but it's the best way to ensure her blog doesn't get hijacked by someone with a dirty mouth.

Another blog that I've found rises above the mainstream is Dooce by Heather B. Armstrong. She actually lost a job because of blogging, but now supports her family through it (she has a gazillion readers who buy her t-shirts, calendars, books, and click on the online ads). Frequently profane (be warned!), she talks about the joys and disappointments of everyday life. She also posts fabulous photos of her dogs and her child (I actually heard about Dooce from Eileen). Not for the timid, but always entertaining.

ThePioneerWoman is a former city girl now living on a ranch in Oklahoma. Like Dooce, she takes beautiful photos of her children and her dogs (and the cattle). She also has recipes! It's a nice way to live vicariously through someone else.

If you're looking for a woman who's blogging about any topic on earth, you should go to Blogher and search their directory.

And if you'd like to keep up with what the entire Blogosphere is saying on any subject, I highly recommend setting up your own Bloglines account. It allows you to search for blogs using keywords you're interested in (a city, a person, a sport, etc.) and then when you find a blog you like, you can subscribe to it and organize the blogs by subject matter (look for the "Easy Subscribe Bookmarklet" on the left-hand side and follow the simple directions). Then, instead of spending half your life reading random blogs, you subscribe to the ones you know are good, and then just go directly to your Bloglines folders. It's like having your own personal assistant who files reading material for you.

Reading blogs can be the biggest waste of time, or the greatest joy, if you find one you can relate to. There's a blog out there for everyone. And if there's not… start writing one!

- Robyn
