January 13, 2010
That's Entertainment!  
     

Things are already happening at the Long Center this year. Since I work for the government, I couldn't help noticing that a show called The Capitol Steps will be here January 29. This group of former Congressional staffers have put together this political satire that targets both political parties!

And on January 19 the Ballet Folklorico de Mexico de Amalia Hernandez will bring the colorful traditional dances of Mexico's regions to the center. For more information about both programs, check the Long Center.

But creams aren't enough for a beautiful skin  
     

Everydayhealth.com reminds us that a good anti-aging diet includes anti-oxidants. As a reminder that means colorful berries, broccoli, spinach, garlic, beans, tea, red wine, tomatoes, yellow and orange root vegetables, nuts, salmon, and water.


    
A Grand Affair Group Show  
     

Wally Workman Gallery will be showing a group of popular artists such as Jan Heaton with all works being priced below $1,000. January 9-30, 1202 West 6th St. You may preview the show at the Wally Workman Web site


    
First Wine Trail of the year  
     

The Wine Lovers' Trail will be held February 5-7 & 12-14 by the 25 members of the Texas Hill Country Wineries. Tickets are $35 person or $60 per couple. Includes one set of tastes at each winery and a box of chocolates. Or if you plan to attend more trails, you may want to consider the $149 pass to all five trails.
