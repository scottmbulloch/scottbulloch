December 30, 2009
     
   
Favorite Celebrations  
     

If you're energetic, head to First Night Austin. A family event downtown with lots of music, fireworks, lights, art!

Primizie Osteria will feature a three course, local Tuscan menu for $45 on New Year's Eve. Everything from house-made potato gnocchi to chianti-expresso braised beef and warm lemon yogurt cake. Seatings at 7 pm & 8:30 pm. Reservations required. 236-0088 x 2

New Year's Eve dinner at TRIO at the Four Seasons featuring six foods that are believed to increase luck. A midnight drawing for a stay at the Four Seasons! $95

And, of course, the Austin Symphony Pops Concert featuring the Duke Ellington Orchestra with Freda Payne.

I haven't been to this one but it sure sounds interesting: Gruene Hall (oldest dance hall in Texas) will have a New Year's party with Delbert McClintock $75

How about seeing Avatar at the IMAX theater at the Bob Bullock Museum on New Year's Eve?

OR stay home with a bottle of sparkly and someone to kiss at midnight!
