Which reminds me…white rice vs. brown rice  
     

We've always heard that brown rice is better for you than white rice and here's a study to prove it. The Harvard School of Public Health found that people who ate at least five servings of white rice per week had a 17 percent greater risk of developing diabetes than those who consumed less than one serving per month. The study also found that consuming two or more servings of brown rice per week was associated with an 11 percent lower risk of developing type 2 diabetes than those who ate less than one serving a month. Thanks for spotting this, Alegria!
