Readers' Recommended Web sites  
     

If you have hard-to-fit feet such as very narrow feet, try Holly Fay's go-to Web site, www.shoebuy.com. Free shipping and returns. Sort of like zappos.com. They also have www.bagsbuy.com with all sorts of bags from handbags to overnight bags.

Eating right can be fun with Hungry Girl. Popular dishes made more nutritious, diet and nutrition news, and a daily email with tips. Thanks to Jill Turlington!

Several readers have mentioned www.Groupon.com. Sort of like LivingSocial Deals that we talked about earlier but even better. I just bought a coupon for $25 worth of food at Threadgill's for $12. Good businesses and good deals.


    
This is my event…chocolate & wine!  
     

On September 24-26, historic Salado hosts the Chocolate & Wine Weekend with 12 events. I'm interested in the Chocolate Trail on Sat the 25th from 2-5pm. Five stops with chocolate specialties and wine ($24.95). The brunch ($65) on Sunday at 11 am offers your choice of three restaurants serving 4-6 course offerings. How about Eggs Benedict with Crabcakes, Watermelon Mint Salad with Cilantro, Feta and Balsamic Glaze, and Grilled Beef Tenderloin Fillets with Chocolate/Merlot Sauce.


    
Update from the Farmer's Market  
     

I am now able to walk through the Farmer's Market and pick out fresh and homemade foods…hurrah! I go to the one at Barton Creek Mall (9-1 on Saturday morning). There are some new vendors and some old friends.

There are two jam makers that are incredible…Star Farmers Market from Rockdale and Confituras in Austin. I tried the Wild Hog Plum Jelly from Star…yummy…a little more "plummy" than ordinary plum jelly. And the Homegrown Tomato Jam from Confituras is so rich it's almost a chutney. Incredible! A touch of balsamic vinegar may be the reason…anyway, perfect for fall. Since they use seasonal produce, the same jams aren't always available. The popular cardamom apple butter will be there but the lavender peach jam will probably be gone.

The egg scare has made me more determined to buy from local, organic sources like Vital Farm. There are brown and blue eggs available (Don't ask me which come from which types of hens). Their "girls" are pasture-raised and look happy to me! Remember free-range is not the same as pasture-raised. Free-range chickens have limited access to outdoor areas and most don't even use it. Vital Farm eggs are also available at other locations such as Whole Foods.
