Love those Latin sounds!  
     
Tiempo Libre, the Cuban group known for Latin jazz and Afro-Cuban rhythms, will be in Austin this Friday, October 21, for one night at the Dell Concert Hall with the Austin Symphony Orchestra. This group is one of the hottest Latin groups. To hear their sound go to http://www.amazon.com/Bach-Havana-Tiempo-Libre/dp/B001V732WY/ref=ntt_mus_ep_dpi_1. For tickets go to http://www.austinsymphony.org/events/tiempo-libre/

    
Artisans' Market  
     
Williams-Sonoma is bringing artisanal products to you with the opportunity to talk to the producers. The October event featured Confituras. Arte y Chocolate, Round Rock Honey, and more. Every third Saturday of the month (except December) at the Arboretum Williams Sonoma.

Wine and Music at Dry Comal Creek  
     
The Austin Wine & Music Festival and Drew Kennedy have teamed up with Dry Comal Creek Vineyards in New Braunfels to put together an afternoon of music and wine to celebrate the release of Drew's fifth album and his first novel, Fresh Water In the Salton Sea.

Oct 23, 2 pm, Dry Creek Vineyards, $30 (includes the album, novel, one wine glass, and one glass of wine). For tickets go to http://www.drycomalcreek.com/

    
Interested in natural wines?  
     
Alice Feiring, natural wine authority will read from her latest book at Vino, Vino (4119 Guadalupe) on Monday, October 23, 5:30- 7 pm. We're talking no sulfites, low alcohol. The Bellotti "Semplicemente Vino" ("Simply Wine") 100% Cortese by Cascina degli Ulivi is made using chemical-free farming, natural native yeasts, and NO ADDITION of sulfites at bottlings.
