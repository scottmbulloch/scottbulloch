Her take on cranberries for Thanksgiving		  
     

Hannah's Red Mold
A sparkling concoction to add the vibrant color and flavor of cranberries to your holiday table… get ready for it to disappear!

  • 2 10-oz. packages of frozen strawberries in syrup
  • 1 small can crushed pineapple, minus all but 1 T. juice
  • 1 can congealed cranberry sauce
  • 1 4-serving package of strawberry-flavored Jello
  • 1 packet unflavored gelatin
  • 1 c. water, divided
  • 1-2 T. mayonnaise for greasing mold

Prepare a ring mold or other suitable pan—I use a ring mold that is 9" across (including the center hole) and 2" high; it holds exactly 6 cups of liquid when filled to the very top. Grease your chosen pan with the mayonnaise, spreading it evenly to lightly coat the entire inner surface of the pan. Do not substitute a non-stick spray, for example, because the mayonnaise gives a wonderful creamy taste to the finished mold. Set prepared pan aside. Partially thaw the strawberries and have ready.

In a large saucepan, mash cranberry sauce, adding three-fourths cup of boiling water. Stir and simmer for about 5 minutes, until smooth. Add the flavored Jello; stir well and then remove pan from heat. In a small cup, dissolve the packet of unflavored gelatin in one-fourth cup cold water; allow to sit for about 5 minutes. Add this to the pan and stir to dissolve well. Add the fruit and juices to the pan. Stir to blend well.

Pour into the prepared mold, cover with plastic wrap and chill in the refrigerator until set, at least several hours or overnight. Can be made a few days ahead and held in the pan in fridge. When ready to unmold, prepare a large serving plate that is at least 2 inches larger in diameter than your mold. Cover entire surface of serving plate with washed and well-dried whole red-tipped lettuce leaves. Run a thin knife around edge of mold to help it separate from the pan. Have ready a clean cloth spread out on counter for blotting bottom of mold. Submerge mold in basin of very warm water for about 20 seconds, being very careful not to allow any water to get in the pan. Lift mold out of water and lightly blot bottom of pan so as not to drip excess water onto serving plate. Quickly invert mold onto lettuce-lined plate and allow the congealed salad to release (you will hear the tell-tale "plop" as it drops out of the pan). Decorate the plate any way you like for serving: I use fresh orange slices, kiwis and grapes around the edge and fill the center cavity with black olives. Serves up to 16. (Adapted from…my mom!)
