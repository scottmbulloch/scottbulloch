Hair Tricks to Look Younger  
     
Four simple things from Health.com for a more youthful look:
  1. Fake fullness - use several colors to give depth, but keep them just one or two shades apart. Makes thin hair look fuller.
  2. That touch of gold - Warm hues like honey reflect light. Cool, ashy tones wash your skin out. (Note: Don't get carried away with the gold, however)
  3. Shine! - Hair has to be healthy. Restore luminosity with a silicone-based spray or serum before blow-drying and a monthly silicone mask. (Note:I haven't tried it, but I understand that Pureology has one; just don't use too much or you'll get build up.)
  4. Embrace your natural hue - Stay within a few shades of your natural color when coloring your hair a darker shade. Not harsh!
To read the entire article, go to Health.com.

And if your hair is fine...

Let's talk about Volume! If you have fine hair, this is a serious subject. John Frieda has a new line of products to pump up the volume of your hair... Luxurious Volume. There is a mousse, a root booster, and a hair spray. I've tried the mousse and really like it. Lucky Magazine recommends the root booster. $7 at drugstores.

Two other great products are Phyto's Phytovolume Actif, which is spritzed at the roots for volume without weight, and Aveda's Phomollient styling mousse.

    
Pink Champagne  
     
Not to drink this time. Laura Mercier has a flattering lipstick called Pink Champagne, which has become famous because it was recommended in How Not to Look Old. BTW I love this book. The tips are good for any age!

Speaking of Laura M, she has some great light fragrances that you could wear at work (Please no strong perfume in an office!). Eau de Lune, which is a light rose, and L'heure Magique, a name chosen by friend Sarah Jessica Parker.

And for fall you might try Neroli by Laura Mercier. David Yurman is coming out with a new fragrance but it is not available yet... should be spicy.
