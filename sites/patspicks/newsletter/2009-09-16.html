Biological Agers  
     

The health problems we have as we get older aren't the problems per se; the biological mechanisms that cause them in the first place are. Drs. Roizen and Oz in their new book list the major aging problems, what they cause, and what to do about them.

Bad Genes and Short Telomeres

Responsible for: Memory Problems
You can't control your genes but you can control how they're expressed. Take telomeres — the tips of your chromosomes. With general wear and tear, they can shorten and fray, causing age-related health issues, such as memory loss.
Action Plan: Help keep your telomeres — and memory — sharp with stress reduction, brain games and regular exercise

Declining Defenses

Responsible for: Weakened Immunity
We're also concerned with chronic infections; they're the ones that can trigger an inflammatory response that ages your entire system.
Action Plan: Try chi-gong (also spelled qigong), a series of meditative movements that help calm the immune system to reduce chronic inflammation.

Toxins

Responsible for: Cancer, Breathing Problems
Some of the toxins make themselves obvious (for instance, they smell bad), but many are colorless or odorless, so you may not notice that they're seeping into your world.
Action Plan: Don't live in fear of every chemical, but be aware of your environment, and make it as healthy as you can.

Disuse Atrophy

Responsible for: Bone Weakening
It's the use-it-or-lose-it theory of aging. And it's true for your memory, your sex organs, your muscles, and especially your bones.
Action Plan: Strengthen your bones by walking every day and getting plenty of calcium and vitamin D.
