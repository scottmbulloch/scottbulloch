Evite Your Friends to Your Next Party  
     
When it comes to planning a party, online invitation services like Evite, Socializr, and MyPunchbowl have almost made stationery, ink, and stamps obsolete.

Evite, at 10 years old, is the grande dame of online invitations, with millions of customers using it every day. Getting an "evite" has become part of American slang, just like "googling" someone on the Internet (which Google, by the way, would like to stop).

The power of using an online invitation is hard to ignore. Everyone who receives it can see who's coming to the party. Responses become part of the invitation, so as more people open the e-mail and see who's coming - and they say how excited they are - the more others do the same. Some say the competition they feel, to either come up with a pithy response or a jealousy-inducing regret (for example, "Sorry, I'd love to be there, but we're going to Paris that weekend"), is part of the fun of receiving online invitations.

Invitation add-ons are designed to help hosts and guests alike, be it location maps, links to gift registries, and requests for contributions to cover party costs. Evite also comes with "drink calculators" and "budget estimators" to help hosts figure out how much chardonnay they'll need to buy and how much the shindig is going to affect the pocketbook.

All of the websites are very easy to use.

The reasons to use e-mailed invitations are many - they're free, they're creative, and they help people connect. Get out there and start eviting your friends and family. Another bonus... you'll know exactly who got the invitation and just didn't bother to reply. No more blaming it on the post office
  1. Create an account (generally your name, password, and e-mail address).
  2. Enter your friends names and e-mail addresses. If you have more than a few, you can usually import them from an Excel spreadsheet or your Outlook contacts folder if you use Microsoft. If you have friends from several different social circles and you'll be inviting them to different events, you can put them into different "groups" that make inviting some - and not all - much easier.
  3. Choose a template for your invitation (this is the fun part). There are hundreds of designs to choose from to match any type of party you're throwing, including designs for baby showers, birthday parties, holiday events, and book club gatherings. If you want to create something really unique, select a background-only design and upload your own photos or artwork to decorate the invitation.
  4. Customize the template with the details of your event. There are boxes to fill in that make this easier so you don't accidentally forget to put the date or time of the party. You can also customize the type of reply a person gives - for example, if you're throwing a wedding shower, people choose "I Do!" as a "yes" reply.
  5. Preview the invitation before sending it. This is like looking in a full-length mirror before going out. Make sure you've spelled everything wright, I mean right.
  6. Hit send.

    
Saber Toothed Cat  
     
A saber-toothed cat guards the entrance to UT's Texas Memorial Museum! The cast-bronze statue arrived last fall and depicts a large male saber-toothed cat, 1.25 times life size. A donation by Sarah and Ernest Butler made this addition possible.

John Maisano, exhibit designer at the museum, created the statue, and it was cast by the Deep in the Heart Art Foundry in Bastrop. The foundry has become a leader in the bronze casting industry and attracts clients from around the nation.

If you haven't been to the museum in a while, you will be amazed by the exhibits! Four floors of exhibits from prehistoric animal life to an exploration of biodiversity. Have family coming for Easter weekend? Although they will be closed for Easter Sunday, they will be open Saturday. Free admission. It would make a great trip for kids (of any age!) More information on website.

2400 Trinity St. on the UT Campus
