Long Term Care: Option for Longevity

Oscar winning actress Bette Davis knew what she was talking about when she said that that "old age is not for sissies." Preparing for it, however, is as the English say, "a bully idea."

Women today - young professionals, many business owners, experienced mothers and proud grandmothers - all lead very busy lives.

With taking care of children, parents, households, careers, community activities and all of the daily challenges of day-to-day living, it is easy to feel overwhelmed and put off planning for the future. Many Americans underestimate the risk of becoming disabled and have misconceptions about who will pay for Long Term Care. But just as they plan for their child's education or retirement, they need to plan for a way to pay for long term care needs.

Many may ask, "Who is going to take care of me?" The younger you are, the less likely you are to identify with this issue. Having a parent or grandparent who needs care may be the closest you have ever come to experiencing the need for long term care.

There are three ways to fund your care:
  1. Personal Assets
  2. Medicaid
  3. Long Term Care Insurance
There will be following articles discussing more details on your long term care, whether at-home care is desirable, or if you might consider an assisted living facility.

Fundamentally speaking, it is asset protection for what we all hope to avoid but know may very well occur.
