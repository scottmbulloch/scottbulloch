Help with the grocery list from Food on the Table  
     
Jan Hesselsweet has discovered a Web site in her wellness group that is really interesting. Food on the Table helps with recipes and cost-cutting shopping for healthy foods...even making the grocer list. You select the grocery stores you prefer and the types of foods you want to buy. The Web site offers recipes using those foods and where they are on sale. You almost have to try it to see what I mean. I was hesitant but really liked it after I tried it.

    
Speaking of food...How to handle leftovers  
     
Always a good reminder, especially with a big Thanksgiving meal coming up. Joyce Moeller found this:
  1. Refrigerate leftovers within two hours of purchase or preparation. Be extra careful about getting meat and dairy products into the fridge promptly, and make sure they are properly sealed.
  2. Keep refrigerated leftovers for about three to five days.
  3. Divide large amounts of leftovers between several clean, shallow storage containers (1-2 inches deep). Make sure storage lids seal tightly, or cover the container with aluminum foil or plastic wrap. Place storage containers at least 2 inches apart in the refrigerator — this allows cold air to circulate.
  4. Keep cooked lean meat and meat dishes for three to four days in the refrigerator and two to three months in the freezer. Raw beef can be stored for six months to a year in a freezer set below 0°F.
  5. Keep cooked lean poultry and poultry dishes? Three to four days in the refrigerator and four to six months in the freezer set below 0°F. Raw poultry can be stored for nine months to a year in a freezer set below 0°F.
  6. Keep the refrigerator at 40°F.
