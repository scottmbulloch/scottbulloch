What not to buy organic  
     
We've seen lists of foods and products that should be bought as organic. Here is a list from Health magazine of what doesn't need to be organic:
  • Avocados – The thick skin protects them but make sure you wash the peel before cutting them.
  • Eggs – Research has shown that factory eggs don't have higher quantities of contaminants than organic eggs. (But I still prefer eggs from chickens that have been fed better and treated more humanely)
  • Frozen food in plastic bags – As long as you're not boiling in the bag. Heat leaches chemicals.
  • Spices – The risk is small because the amounts are small.
  • Clothing – Organic cotton is good for the environment but it is unlikely that there are significant amounts of pesticides remaining on the clothing.
