Guy Forsyth and Carolyn Wonderland Holidays by Candlelight  
     
Guy Forsyth and Carolyn Wonderland will be at the Long Center with their bands to bring back the magic of their annual holiday shows. Guy is known for his unique Americana/Blues sound and Carolyn is known to have the soulful vocals of Janis and the guitar slinging skills of Stevie Ray.

Go a little early and enjoy several of the displays from the Trail of Lights that are at the Long Center.
Wednesday, December 21 at 7:30pm
Thursday, December 22 at 7:30pm
Friday, December 23 at 7:30pm

    
1611 W. 12th Street  
     
(around the corner from Jeffrey's and Zocalo)
This yard is crammed full of lights and moving figures. Willis, the homeowner, puts them out every night and takes them back in every night. And you'll see Willis out in his yard greeting folks and handing out candy canes.

    
Circle C  
     
Circle C has some great lights but it's hard to tell you where they are because there will 2 or 3 displays in a block but then you need to drive a few blocks to find another group. I took several streets east off of Escarpment and found some great ones.

    
And in San Antonio...  
     
If you live in SA you probably know these locations. If you plan to be in SA check them out!

The City of Windcrest (off I-35 as you come into SA) is probably the gold standard for neighborhood lights. Prepare for crowds!

My favorite is The University of the Incarnate Word (4301 Broadway). Lights drip from huge trees that cover the campus. It looks like a fairyland!

    
Hot Buttered Rum  
     
I've been looking for a recipe for Hot Buttered Rum that sounds like the incredible concoction served at the Four Seasons, but none sound right. In addition, they usually make large quantities. But if you get to the Four Seasons, that stuff will take you to a warm, fuzzy place!
