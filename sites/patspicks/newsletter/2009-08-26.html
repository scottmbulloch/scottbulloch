Bats and Burgers!  
     

A little spot about 10 miles south of Fredericksburg is larger than Austin's bat colony. Over 3 million bats can be seen leaving a tunnel near the Alamo Springs Café. By the way, the café has incredible burgers and daily dinner specials. Live entertainment on Saturday nights. Bekki Lammert was there recently and says the food is wonderful. She read about it in a book about great hill country cafes called The Texas Hill Country: A Food and Wine Lover's Paradise. I've lived in Texas all my life and had never heard of some of these places. Some really interesting finds.


    
Austin Artist and the Ritzenhoff Collection  
     

Congratulations to Austin artist Allison Gregory, who was recently selected to be one of the artists on the 2009 Ritzenhoff Collection. More than 2,000 artists compete each year for the honor of having their work on their glassware, tableware, etc. Ritzenhoff is particularly well-known in Europe and New York. Funky, unusual pieces… One of the glasses might make a good holiday gift for the beer drinker in your life!


    
Now you can rent those textbooks!  
     

A teacher told Geri Williams about Chegg, a school/university textbook rental service. Geri rented a textbook for her daughter. The book was sold out at the bookstores but sold for $95 new and $75 used. Geri got it from this Web site for $34 (plus a few bucks to ship). There are several shipping methods depending on how quick you need it. Books may be rented for up to 125 days with an option to buy… or just return them. Geri will let us know how it goes!


    
Bandslam  
     

Go see Bandslam and take a good look at the band that wins the contest in the movie. Edna Butt's son Evan is part of local band, The Daze, that is featured. Congrats!
