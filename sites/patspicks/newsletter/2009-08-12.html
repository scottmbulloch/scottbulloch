When moisturizer isn't enough in the sun  
     

The heat and intense sun make sunscreen a must. Most moisturizers have sunscreen in them, but it is not enough. Look for a sunscreen made specifically for the face; they are lighter and non-greasy. Look for broad spectrum protection (UVA & UVB). I like the one that Clarins (SPF 40) makes because it very light, but Chanel and Dior also make good ones.

Avoid chemical screens with oxybenzone (and over 600 products contain it) and look for those that use minerals to block rays, like titanium dioxide and zinc oxide. Although not conclusive, studies show that oxybenzone is a likely photocarcinogen. Why take a chance? Check WebMD for more info on the research. As far as SPF, my dermatologist said that anything over 30 probably doesn't make a lot of difference.

I'm sure that there are some at the drugstore that are good, but the one I tried was too heavy, more like a body sunscreen. A facial sunscreen goes under makeup, but if I know I'm going to be out in the direct sun I dab a little extra over my makeup. If you have found a less expensive sunscreen that meets the criteria above, would you let me know?


    
Required vaccinations  
     

If you have children or grandchildren returning to school in a few weeks, vaccination requirements have changed and must be completed by the start of school. Five new immunizations have been added. Vaccines affected are Hepatitis A, MMR, Varicella, Tdap, Meningococcal. Check here for all requirements.
