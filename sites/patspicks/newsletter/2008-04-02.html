What would a skin care specialist do?

What would a skin care specialist choose if she could choose one skin care product?

We talked to Nicole Erickson Cato, owner of Frou Frou, a salon specializing in European skincare which combines the elements of Deep Pore Cleansing, Exfoliation, Massage, and balancing the skin on a regular basis.

"My absolute favorite product is hard to choose, with there being so many, but I love the Vitamin ce Ferulic of Skin Ceuticals, and it is for the most part universal for skin that needs to be repaired and protected from photoaging, and sun damage. Most dermatologists will agree with me on this, and Harvard and Duke back up all the science on the performance of this product."

The vitamin C works as an antioxidant to absorb (absorbic acid serum) to a cellular level, and protects each individual cell from breaking down. Combined with the C is another antioxidant, vitamin E for hydration and protection. The ferulic is another antioxidant found in the cell walls of plants, wheat, rice and oats.

This product helps with hyperpigmentation, brown spots, and fine lines and wrinkles.

Touring the Farmer's Market

This is high season for veggies, strawberries, and all kinds of fresh produce and flowers! The Sunset Valley Farmer's Market is brimming with farmers and local artisans every Saturday from 9am-1pm.

First stop is Full Quiver Farms for fresh organic cheeses made by the Sams family... cheddar, mozzarella, feta, and the best selection of yummy cream cheese spreads such as spinach & feta, strawberry, and pineapple.

Next we look for veggies at Flintrock Hill, Gunderman Farms, and Finca Pura Vida Heirloom. The tomatoes and strawberries are red and fragrant now. Try the Swiss Chard or spinach also!

And the asparagus at McKemie Farms is incredible... tiny delicate stalks that are so tender you can eat the whole stalk.

Richardson Farms sells their grass-fed beef and pork... Yes, you can taste the difference, plus it is more nutritious (Check out In Defense of Food).

Oops, don't forget the Round Rock Honey (especially if you have allergies) and canned pickles and salsa at Hill Country Homestyle Canning. Try the Sweet and Spicy pickles... like nothing you've had!

And treat yourself to a bouquet of colorful, organically grown flowers on the way out at Blue House Flower Farm.

In the parking lot of the Toney Burger Center located on the eastbound access road of 290 W between Brodie Ln and Westgate Blvd.
