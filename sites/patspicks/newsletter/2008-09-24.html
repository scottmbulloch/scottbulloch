King Tut is coming!  
     
Opening on October 3, 2008, at the Dallas Museum of Art, Tutankhamun and the Golden Age of the Pharaohs provides insight into the life of Tutankhamun and other royals of the 18th Dynasty (1555-1305 BC). All of the treasures in the exhibition are between 3,300 and 3,500 years old.

Tutankhamun was one of the last kings of Egypt's 18th Dynasty and ruled during a crucial, turmoil-filled period of Egyptian history. The boy king died under mysterious circumstances around age 18 or 19, in the ninth year of his reign (1323 BC).

Tutankhamun and the Golden Age of the Pharaohs offers glimpses of that evolving period. On display are fifty of Tutankhamun's burial objects, including his royal diadem - the gold crown discovered encircling the head of his mummified body that he likely wore as king - and one of the gold and precious stone inlaid canopic coffinettes that contained his mummified internal organs.

More than seventy additional objects from tombs of 18th Dynasty royals, as well as several non-royal individuals, are also exhibited. These stone, faience, and wood pieces from burial sites before Tut's reign give visitors a sense of what the lost burials of other royalty and commoners may have been like.
- text from dallasmuseumofart.org

Tutankhamun and the Golden Age of the Pharaohs will be at the Dallas Museum of Art from October 3, 2008, to May 17, 2009. For more information and to purchase tickets, visit the Dallas Museum of Art website.

    
Target news from Jenn Luin  
     
The new makeup lines were selected by Target to put them directly in competition with Sephora.

The new lines are Pixi by Petra, NP Set by Napoleon Perdis, and JK by Jemma Kidd. Scandalous note: Napoleon was originally sold at Sephora - he pulled his line so he could sell it at Target, hoping to make bigger sales.

However, what I am SO excited about are two accessories collaborations. Sigerson Morrison is doing a small line of shoes, very sparkly and festive - some are VERY young looking, but a few would be a nice shoe for holiday parties for your readers at bargain prices. The other collaboration is a line of handbags by Anya Hindmarch. In my opinion, these look to be the classiest design collaboration Target has done yet. Lots of patent for fall, but classic, ladylike shapes.

They debut online and in stores on October 12, and you'd better believe I'll be there! The big black tote and matching clutch are the ones I'm eyeing.
