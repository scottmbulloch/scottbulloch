Breast Cancer and Diet  
     

From Dr. Andre Weil (thanks to Joyce Moeller for sending). Dr. Weil suggests the following eating habits as one of the ways to help prevent breast cancer:

  1. Keep your fat content low. Below 25 percent of your daily calories is ideal.
  2. Avoid animal fats, polyunsaturated fats (many vegetable oils), and hydrogenated oils (margarines and vegetable shortenings)
  3. Use healthy fats: monounsaturates such as extra virgin olive oil, freshly ground flaxseed and oily fish such as wild Alaskan salmon and sardines (which are rich in omega-3 fatty acids)
  4. Include whole soy products in your diet. Soy foods contain many cancer-protective substances, including isoflavones. One to two servings of whole soy-based foods a day.
  5. Avoid alcohol. Even in modest amounts, alcohol consumption is associated with an increased risk of breast cancer. (Pat's note: Surely, this can't be right!)
  6. Minimize your consumption of conventionally raised meat, poultry, eggs, and dairy products. Replace with organic, hormone-free versions and use sparingly.
  7. Eat more fruits and vegetables!
