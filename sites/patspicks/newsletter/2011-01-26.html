Web site roundup  
     
Some of these I've mentioned before but they have become so popular I thought they deserved another look-see!

Groupon
Great deals of all kinds but especially restaurants. Usually you pay about half for double the amount of food. For example, pay $10 for $20 of food. Some of my favorites have been Threadgill's, Blue Star, Galaxy Cafe. Deals are available for a limited number of days each week. No fee to sign up at www.groupon.com. And now you can get an app for your smart phone that stores your Groupons so that you don't even have to print out the coupons!

Open Table
I had forgotten about this one until Eliza Morehead reminded me. You can check for available reservations at www.opentable.com. Austin is not a featured city so you need to go to the Texas option and select Austin. Reviews and locations are included.

Texasmonthly.com
Nancy Kurio gets a newsletter from Texas Monthly that lists what's going on in surrounding communities. I signed up for several of their newsletters. You can also go to their Web site for recipes, travels, etc. at www.texasmonthly.com. Newsletters are at the top of the page.

    
For the adventurous shopper  
     
If you're looking for deals and willing to work for them, here are some things to try.

Le Garage Sale will be held January 29 (10am - 5pm) & 30 (noon - 5pm) at Palmer Events Center. Admission is $10 (cash only). Actually, many of the merchants accept only cash also. Usually no dressing rooms so dress so that you try on in public. Stores tend to be the trendier independent boutiques. You can find the deals but you also have to be lucky!

Dale Coker reminds us that Dillard's has converted their Highland Mall store into a discount store. Discounts are usually about 70 percent but they have sales that may have an additional 20 percent off. Again, you can find deals but you have to know your merchandise. Saw some jackets that were good possibilities.

    
La Sylphide  
     
Don't fight the restaurant crowds on Valentine's Day, go to the ballet! Ballet Austin will present the classic story of a forest fairy who uses her magical gifts to attract a young Scotsman on the eve of his nuptials. February 11 & 12 at 8pm and February 13 at 3pm. Tickets range from $94-$24 at www.balletaustin.org.

    
Wines of Patagonia dinner  
     
With all the talk about great wines coming from Argentina now, thought you might be interested in a wine dinner at La Sombra Bar & Grill that benefits the Wine and Food Foundation. Check out the menu at the Web site and make reservations. $65

    
Hermes Birkin tote wait list  
     
Better instructions for getting one of the totes mentioned last week: Go to the Web site and order and pay for the tote. They will put you on the wait list and notify you when they ship...around March now.
