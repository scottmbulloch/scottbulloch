Conversation with a dermatologist  
     
Lauren Carlson, who was an intern with Pat's Picks, wrote this for a previous edition but with summer right around the corner I thought it was worth repeating.
Lauren Carlson (Pat's Picks): What is the biggest skin problem you see among women in their 40's and 50's? What is it attributed to?
Dr. Adrienne Feasel: Photoaging - Many women complain of dark spots and wrinkles due to chronic sun damage.
LC: Is there a common misconception that women tend to have about skincare?
AF: "My makeup has an SPF so I'm protected." Women often do not wear the amount of foundation needed to achieve that SPF, and they do not apply makeup to other exposed areas, such as the neck, arms, and décolletage. An even more common misconception is that you don't need sun protection daily. People underestimate the effects of cumulative daily exposure (running errands, driving). We have all been guilty of staying outside longer than planned.
LC: What are some measures that all women should take during the summer?
AF: All women need to use sun protection — year-round. It is important to reapply after 2 hours or after swimming during times of prolonged exposure.
LC: Any measures specific to different age groups?
AF: I believe women of all ages can benefit from daily sunscreen, topical retinoids or alpha hydroxy acids, and antioxidants – whether it is to correct or prevent photodamage.
LC: Best sunscreen?
AF: I like products that contain zinc oxide or titanium dioxide as they cover a broad spectrum of UVA and UVB wavelengths and are unlikely to irritate even sensitive skin. Helioplex and Mexoryl also provide broad UVA/UVB coverage. Other methods of photo-protection include UPF (Ultraviolet protection factor) hats and clothing. RIT makes Sunguard, a laundry additive that imparts sun protection to your own clothes in the wash.
LC: Best SPF for face? Body?
AF: I recommend SPF 30 most often, but higher SPF may be used for long periods of sun exposure. Remember – SPF only describes UVB protection, so be sure a product covers UVA and UVB.
LC: If you already have sun damage, what can you do about it?
AF: Over the counter measures include products containing retinol or glycolic acid. A dermatologist can evaluate the skin and prescribe other retinoids, bleaching creams, and other products tailored to individual needs. Chemical peels and several lasers can be used, depending on the type and severity of photodamage.
Now you are armed with everything you need in order to fight those pesky rays all year long and maintain a beautiful complexion. And just in case you need a little extra motivation to put that sunscreen on each morning, check out this fact provided by WebMD: One woman at age 40 who has protected her skin from the sun actually has the skin of a 30-year-old! If that's not motivation, I don't know what is.

Dr. Feasel completed her dermatology residency at the University of Texas Health Science Center and M.D. Anderson Cancer Center, where she served as chief resident during her final year. She is Board Certified by the American Academy of Dermatologyand can be contacted at Ladera Park Dermatology in Austin at (512)345-3599.
