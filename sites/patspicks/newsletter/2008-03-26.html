Happy Birthday to the Austin Symphony Designer Showhouse!  
     
For 25 years the Showhouse has brought Austin's best builders, designers and suppliers together to showcase the newest home design and décor. With one of only four showhouses in Texas, Austin is fortunate to have such a great resource for ideas!
8909 Calera Drive
in Barton Creek near Fazio Canyons
Built by Darin Gray Home Builders
Design liaisons: Joe Burke and Mark Smith of The Design Center
If you can't wait until the Showhouse officially opens and you love food, you need to get to the Preview Party on April 3. See the Showhouse without the crowds while you munch on...

Mesquite Grilled Beef Tenderloin, Shrimp Corn Cakes, and Mango, Brie, and Avocado Quesadillas... Yum!!

Tickets: $90 Call 477-1577 for information.
Hours: Wednesday - Saturday 10am-5pm; Sunday noon-5pm
Tickets: $15 In partnership with Southern Accents and Texas Home & Living

Over 20 designers will show their best designs for the Foyer, Living Room, Study, Courtyard, Balcony, Master Suite, Guest Suite, and much more...

    
Notes from Readers  
     
Thanks to Susan for sending the list of exciting events coming up in Dripping Springs (see "Events"). By the way, the cooking classes at Juniper Hills Farms sell out quickly. We only listed the ones that still had openings. These are VERY popular.

Readers have more favorite Happy Hours:
Several people mentioned Blue Star Cafeteria at 4800 Burnet Road
Happy Hour Mon-Fri 4pm-7pm

Grilled Chicken Quesadillas with lime marinated chicken, spinach, mushrooms & Asadero cheese ($3.49) or two Blue Lump Crab Cakes with Cajun remoulade sauce ($4.39)

Vin in Jefferson Square has a nice Happy Hour. Mon-Fri Daily? 4pm-7pm. Just make sure you sit at the bar. Appetizers are not half price at the tables.

Regarding the potentially harmful use of plastics, especially when heated, one reader buys the waxed paper commercial folds to cover food being reheated in the microwave. Self-serve bakeries often use these for selecting baked goods. Large discounters usually carry them, although I didn't spot them at Costco.
