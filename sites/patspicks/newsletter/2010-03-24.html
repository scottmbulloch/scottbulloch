Or a green molded-glass bunny filled with jelly beans from Crate and Barrel. $6.95


Or a rustic bunny from the nursery at Breed & Co.that can be used for Easter and later in the garden. $20.95


Or if you're feeling adventurous, follow Margaret Patterson's lead and make cascarones (confetti eggs) with the kids or grandchildren. Apparently, the guys love to blow the eggs out and the girls like to decorate the eggs. Here are the instructions.

  1. Poke (with something like an ice pick or nail) two holes in the ends of the eggs; the hole at the base should be a little larger. Break the yoke with the pick and blow (through the smaller hole) the egg out. Some folks just make a bigger hole with a knife on one end and pour the egg out.
  2. Rinse the inside of the shell and allow to dry.
  3. Color or decorate the shells with dye, markers, or paint.
  4. When dry, fill about half full with confetti.
  5. Put a thin line of glue around the larger hole and cover with a piece of tissue paper. Allow to dry.
  6. Crack over someone's head, not on the head!
