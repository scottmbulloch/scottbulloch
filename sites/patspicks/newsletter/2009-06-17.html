Margaret's Musings  
     

A year ago when my 18-year-old goddaughter had trouble reading a note from me because it was written in cursive, I blogged on this Web site, asking "Whatever Happened to Cursive?"

This month an answer came from an unexpected source. In a recent dust-up between Texas Land Commissioner Jerry Patterson and state Representative Wayne Christian, R-Center, over a bill related to beach property and beach houses, the always colorful Patterson used some even more colorful language to describe Christian's bill.

In the June 8 issue of the Austin American Statesman, Christian is quoted as having railed about Patterson's "cursive language."

Ah, now I know what happened to cursive. It has evolved from a form of writing to a manner of speaking.
