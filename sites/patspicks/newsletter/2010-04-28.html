Run for the Roses!  
     

May 1 is the famed horse racing tradition of the Kentucky Derby. My daughter is a jockey so I probably follow these events more than most folks. Besides, I love the pageantry and food associated with the Derby. Get in the mood with a Kentucky Derby Pie.

You can also order one from Soup Peddler for delivery next week (May 3-6).

This delicious Kentucky bourbon chocolate walnut pie is traditionally served at the annual Kentucky Derby Horse race. This pie recipe is similar to the Melrose Inn's famous "Derby Pie," which has been served at the race for over 50 years.

Makes 1 Kentucky Bourbon Chocolate Walnut Pie
Prep Time: 5 minutes
Cook Time: 45 minutes
Total Time: 50 minutes

Ingredients:

  • ½ cup flour
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • ½ cup melted butter
  • 2 tablespoons Kentucky bourbon
  • 1 cup chopped walnuts
  • 1 1/4 cup semi-sweet chocolate chips
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • pinch of salt
  • 1 ready-made piecrust

Preparation:
Preheat oven to 350 degrees F. Combine flour and sugar in a mixing bowl. Add the eggs and butter; mix to combine. Stir in the bourbon, walnuts, chocolate chips, vanilla, and salt. Pour the mixture into the unbaked piecrust. Bake for 40 to 45 minutes. Let cool before slicing.


    
Antibacterial warning  
     

Triclosan is an antibacterial chemical found in lots of our liquid soaps, dishwashing detergents, shaving gels and even things like toys and socks. Recently, the FDA and EPA have decided to take another look at its safety, particularly if it interferes with the endocrine system and whether it creates drug-resistant bacteria. The European Union countries have banned or restricted its use. This Web site lists some of the most common products. Just something to keep your ears open about.
