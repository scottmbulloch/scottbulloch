The Net and Our Pets

Social networking websites, where you can write about your favorite things and show off your connections to friends, are some of the most popular applications on the web.

Millions of people each day log onto MySpace, Facebook and/or LinkedIn and check out their friends' activities. Turns out, people aren't the only ones who are networking. Our furry friends also have websites - Dogster and Catster.

These sites have been around for years, and they continue to grow by the hundreds of "critters" each day. They are operated by the same company and look remarkably similar, except, of course, one site features cats and the other one dogs.

Dogster and Catster allow people to share info on what's unique and special about their pets. They can form groups according to breed, where they're from, what they like to do, and their ailments. You can create a page for a dearly-departed pet and give him/her virtual wings.

When you get tired of networking with real people, Dogster and Catser are a nice walk in the park.
-Robyn

    
     
Wedding Stories

For everyone out there who's addicted to "chick flicks," did you know you can get a weekly dose of goosebumps caused by true love stories from the New York Times' "Weddings and Celebrations" section?

You don't even have to know these people to be entertained by their stories of how they found each other and made it to the altar.

Case in point - this Sunday's article about the New York wedding of Nora Burnett and Brian Abrams. The met at the Colorado wedding of Brooke Borgen, who said, "It was love at first sight and love at last call."
-Robyn
