August 3, 2011
     
Anybody who has a cooler place to go has left town by now! Things don't sound as much fun if it's 105 degrees. SO I am mentioning only things that can be done in air conditioning with minimal effort!


    
Summer favorites cooking classes  
     
Central Market has lots of classes but two that stand out are the Summer Favorites of Rebecca Rather (the pastry queen) including Quail Salad with Peaches & Blackberries and Farm to Market Bread Salad with Heirloom Tomatoes.

Justin Raiford of 34th Street Café offers elegant American bistro food with summertime favorites such as New Orleans-style Barbecued Shrimp & Buttered Grits and Watermelon & Heirloom Tomato Gazpacho.

    
Crazy, Stupid, Love  
     
People are saying this film is a feel-good romantic comedy that is a good reason to spend the afternoon in the cool of a theater with a bag of popcorn. I haven't seen it but watching Steve Carell learning to get back in the dating scene under the tutelage of Ryan Gosling has got to be good!

Folks have also raved about Midnight in Paris, which is still in town. It is a romantic comedy about a family that goes to Paris on business and the experiences there that change their lives. Owen Wilson stars in this Woody Allen film.

By the way, if you loved the book The Help as much as I did you'll be looking for that film August 10.

    
Alexander McQueen at the Met  
     
If you don't think you'll be waiting in line for hours to see the Alexander McQueen exhibit at the Metropolitan Museum of Art in New York, you can order the exhibit catalog at the museum's Web site. McQueen was the cutting edge designer who committed suicide in 2010.
