Washing (or not washing) foods  
     
With all the cooking some of us will be doing (I have restaurant reservations), it might be a good time to review what needs to be washed and what doesn't. There are lots of these list but a recent one by Rachael Ray lists these:

Cantaloupe - Definitely wash! Bugs get the tiny crevices of the skin.
Chicken - Don't wash. Cooking chicken to 165 degrees kills the bad stuff and the juices from the raw chicken could contaminate counters and cooking utensil when washed.
Prewashed lettuce - Don't wash. I have seen other authorities who say was, however.
Organic food - Yes, wash! There are microorganisms that can cause food poisoning that have nothing to do with the use, or not use, of pesticides.
Canned beans - Wash. Most canned beans are loaded with sodium and washing gets rid of 40 percent of the sodium.
Thyme, oregano, and rosemary – Wash. Most people wash the thick-leafeed herbs like parsley but but don't think about the thin-leafed herbs. Microorganisns can hide on them also! Pat dry.
