Gluten-free Recipes  
     
A few weeks ago we were talking about restaurants that offer gluten-free menu items. Cooking instructor Marilyn Scher writes a column for The Jewish Outlook and shared her recipes with us.

CHILI-CHEESE CORNBREAD
While not by any means low-fat, this delicious cornbread gets points for being flourless and, therefore, gluten-free.
Ingredients:
  • 3 ears fresh corn (or 1½ cups frozen corn, thawed)
  • 1 cup yellow cornmeal (not coarse-ground)
  • 2 tsp. kosher salt
  • 3 tsp. Baking powder
  • 1 cup sour cream (regular or light)
  • ¾ cup (1½ sticks) unsalted butter,melted
  • 2 eggs
  • 4 oz. Monterey Jack cheese, cut into very small dice
  • 1 small can diced green chiles
Preparation:
Set oven to 350 degrees. Place all the butter (cut in small pieces)in a nine-inch square pan and place the pan in the oven so the butter will melt; watch carefully, and remove the pan just before all the butter melts, so the butter doesn’t burn. Swirl the pan so the butter coats the entire inside surface, and then set the pan aside. Scrape corn kernels from the cobs and place in a large mixing bowl; discard the cobs. Add all remaining ingredients, and pour in the melted butter from the baking pan. Mix well, and then pour the mixture back into the baking pan. Bake for 45 minutes, or until browned and the tester comes clean. (Adapted from "Beard on Bread," by James Beard)
SPICY BLACK BEAN SOUP
A simpler soup cannot be found: canned beans combined with just a few ingredients result in a truly delicious, satisfying soup - totaling a mere couple of bucks...hear that, students?
Ingredients:
  • 1 T. vegetable oil
  • 1 medium onion, finely minced
  • 1 large clove garlic, smashed and minced
  • 1 small fresh jalapeño pepper (or other mildly hot pepper), seeded and minced
  • 1 tsp. ground cumin
  • 1 tsp. chili powder
  • Pinch dried oregano
  • 2 cans (15 oz. each) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 3 cups water
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 tsp. salt, or to taste
  • A few grinds of black pepper
  • Chopped fresh cilantro
  • minced scallions
  • sour cream for garnish (optional)
Preparation:
Heat the oil in a soup pot, and then add the onion, garlic and jalapeño. Sauté over medium-high heat for five minutes, until softened. Add the seasonings and stir briefly; then add the beans, water and bay leaf. Bring to a simmer, cover the pot and lower the heat so the contents remain at a gentle simmer. Cook for 15 minutes, and then remove the bay leaf. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Puree briefly (not completely smooth, so the mixture retains slightly chunky texture). Reheat the soup before serving. Garnish each serving with minced scallions, some chopped cilantro and a small dollop of sour cream, if desired.
RED PEPPER VINAIGRETTE
A dazzling dressing to make even a simple bowl of mixed greens a standout.
Ingredients:
  • 1 red bell pepper, roasted, peeled and seeds removed
  • 1 T. aged balsamic vinegar
  • 1 small clove garlic, minced
  • Leaves from 2 sprigs fresh basil (optional)
  • Juice of ½ lemon
  • ½ tsp. kosher salt
  • Dash of ground black pepper
  • 5-6 T. best quality olive oil
Preparation:
Combine all ingredients in a blender or food processor. Serve over washed and spun-dry mixed salad greens...equally delicious when drizzled over roasted or mashed potatoes!
FLOURLESS CHOCOLATE-WALNUT COOKIES
A lovely (and gluten-free) little treat, courtesy of pastry chef extraordinaire Francois Payard.
Ingredients:
  • 2 cups walnut halves, lightly toasted and then finely chopped
  • 3 cups confectioner's sugar
  • ½ cup, plus 3 T. unsweetened cocoa powder
  • ¼ tsp. sea salt
  • 4 large egg whites
  • 1½ tsp. pure vanilla extract
Preparation:
Preheat oven to 350 degrees. Lightly grease or line two baking sheets with parchment paper. In a large mixing bowl, combine the cocoa, sugar and salt. Add the chopped walnuts, then the egg whites and vanilla, stirring until mixed well. Drop the batter by spoonfuls onto prepared baking sheets, allowing space between the cookies. Bake until the tops are cracked and glossy, about 12-15 minutes. Allow the cookies to stand on baking sheets for two minutes before transferring to racks to finish cooling. Store the cookies in ab airtight container at room temperature for up to one week.

    
More good news about the Sunshine Vitamin (Vitamin D)  
     
It inhibits the birth of new cancer cells, it encourages existing "rogue" cells to kill themselves off, and it slows the growth of blood vessels that feed growing tumors. And there's growing evidence that the vitamin protects against more than one type of cancer. If you don't eat foods with Vitamin D and don't get much sunshine, the YOU Docs recommend taking 1,000 international units (IU) of D a day -- 1,200 IU if you're over age 60. Check with your doctor.

Four things not to buy at Target  
     
I didn't know there was anything not to buy at Target but here they are! CBS Moneywatch.com says you do do better at other stores on these items:
  1. Furniture
    Target's design edge in categories like clothing and housewares doesn't extend to home furniture.
  2. Movies and Music
    "They can't really beat Amazon or Walmart"
  3. Exercise Equipment
    Target's offerings in this category tend toward the $199 "As Seen on TV" Ab Circle Pro You're generally better off going to a place like The Sports Authority for higher-end exercise equipment. Or join a gym and hire a pro.
  4. Shower Curtains, Some Toys, and Other Products with PVC
    Following a national campaign by the Center for Health, Environment and Justice (CHEJ) and other environmental groups, Target agreed in 2007 to reduce the use of polyvinyl chloride (PVC) plastic in its products and packaging.
Of particular concern are toys not sold under Target's private-label brand; toys sold under Target's brand should generally be PVC-free. And read the labels on plastic shower curtains: Products that contain the recycling symbol with the number '3' in the middle mean they contain PVC.
