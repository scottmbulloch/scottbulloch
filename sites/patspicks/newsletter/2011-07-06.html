POTATO & TOASTED CORN SALAD
Light and creamy; toasting makes the corn and scallions extra sweet!
  • 1 1/2 pounds small red or Yukon Gold potatoes, scrubbed well and then quartered
  • 1 Tbsp. olive oil
  • 2 ears fresh corn
  • 2 large scallions, sliced
  • 1/4 cup buttermilk
  • 1 to 2 Tbsp. good quality mayonnaise
  • 2 tsp. white wine vinegar
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
Place potatoes in large saucepan and cover with cold water. Cover pan and bring to a boil; lower heat and continue simmering until potatoes test done when pierced with a paring knife (about 10 minutes). Drain potatoes and transfer to a shallow bowl or plate and let cool for a few minutes, until they're only slightly warm. Meanwhile, heat a medium skillet and add the olive oil. Cut kernels from corn cobs and add to skillet; cook, covered, over medium-highheat for about two minutes. Add in the scallions, and continue to cook, covered, for another minute. Uncover pan and cook one more minute, or until corn and scallionsare lightly toasted, and season with a little salt and pepper; let cool while you make the dressing. In a large mixing bowl, combine the buttermilk, mayonnaise and wine vinegar. Stir or whisk to blend well, and season with salt and pepper. Add in the partially cooled potatoes, along with the corn and scallions. Toss gently to coat everything well. Place in the refrigerator to chill for at least one hour before serving. Garnish with fresh herbs (parsley,chives, edible blossoms) if desired.The salad keeps well for up to three days in the refrigerator.

WATERMELON, CUCUMBER & TOMATO SALAD
A contrast of gorgeous colors with the pink (or yellow) watermelon, red (or yellow) tomatoes and pale green cucumber — and even more delicious!
  • 2 cups seedless watermelon, cut in large dice (about 1")
  • 4 to 5 Roma tomatoes, cut in eighths
  • 1 large cucumber, peeled wita vegetable peeler
  • About 1/4 cup fresh basil leaves, cut in fine "chiffonade," or slivers
  • About 2 Tbsp. olive oil
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Touch of kosher salt
  • A few grinds of black pepper
In a large bowl, combine the cut-up watermelon and tomatoes. Slice the cucumber lengthwise into quarters, and scoop out any large seeds; cut the quartered cucumber into small dice and add to the watermelon and tomatoes. Add the slivered basil leaves, the olive oil and the lime juice; toss gently to mix everything well. Season with a little kosher salt and pepper, and serve.
