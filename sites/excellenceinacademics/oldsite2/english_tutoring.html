English Tutoring

(Any one option is at a rate of $50 dollars/hour)

Curriculum Enrichment (CE) Offers:

  • A comprehensive reinforcement of the curriculum and homework.
  • Reinforcement of prior knowledge for tests.
  • Worksheets based on homework and curriculum, so that skills are applied and reinforced.
  • Assessments that measure the growth of the objectives taught.
  • Grades 4-12

Writing (W) Offers:

  • Models and practice for any type and level of writing.
  • It can range from developing a paragraph with its essential elements to writing research papers that emphasize academic discussions and correct MLA format.
  • It will focus on the writing that the parent wants the student to master.
  • It can reinforce the writing being taught at school.
  • It can introduce the writing the student will be expected to master in future classes.
  • It can focus on writing for a specific purpose, such as reports, essays, college essay applications, or state and standardized writing tests, such as the AIMS, SAT, or ACT.
  • Grades 4-12

Literature Enrichment (LE) Offers:

  • Review of literary elements and genres in literature that the author employs.
  • It includes defining, identifying, and applying those elements in the student’s curriculum, or it can be offered as a separate curriculum.
  • Practice in analytical thinking.
  • This is excellent for preparing students in analyzing and writing about literature.
  • Grades 6-12

Grammar/Vocabulary (G/V) Offers:

  • It can be offered as a reinforcement of the school’s curriculum, or it can be offered as its own enrichment course.
  • It assures that the student retains the vocabulary at the current school year, it can introduce sophisticated vocabulary that the student will learn in progressive grades.
  • Practice and homework will be given so that the student masters using vocabulary that sounds fresh and natural in any writing situation.
  • Reinforcement and practice of the grammatical skills being taught in school is given.
  • Introduces new grammatical concepts.
  • Reviews the parts of speech.
  • Students will apply the skills to their writing so that connections to how grammar is used in their everyday context can be seen.
  • Grades 3-12
