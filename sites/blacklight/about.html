Colors are my passion – the interplay, the drama of lighting, the infinite combinations. What better to explore this sense than photography, travel and writing. From the simple days of a Konica in Europe in 1964 to the amazing world of digital imagery at present, the world has provided me an incredible universe of emerging nations, traditional arts, the patterns and detail specific to each country. Capturing the natural essence of both moment and place is paramount in my work. "Street Photography" on a global plateau.

The past year has found me exploring Egypt in depth from the majesty of the Nile to the fabled Western Oases; travel into the Eastern Kham regions of China and Tibet where the nomadic life is meeting the 21st century head-on; Antarctica and the countries of the Southern Oceans.

January 2007 offered the opportunity to wander the Kmer Kingdoms of Cambodia and Laos. Magnificent archaeological wonders paralleled by the images of everyday life of Tonle Sap and the Mekong. The balance of 2007 finds me, at this point, primarily at home doing some desperately needed work on the detail of the photo catalogue. But my calendar is always open.

The future… as you can tell, anything is possible. So many unique opportunities on distant horizons that the list of places I want to go and explore continually grows.

Credits include familiar magazines (Specialty Travel, Archaeology, Conde Nast Traveler, House Beautiful, National Geographic, Travel & Leisure); textbooks; travel guides (Lonely Planet); newspapers (UNM Press, NY Times); advertising agencies; commercial applications (Pei Wei Asian Diner) and probono work for nonprofit causes. Tour escort for small specialized travel in China, Turkey, SE Asia, India and N Africa. Assignment work welcome.
